Overview

TYDES helps users organize debt and liability obligations across linked financial accounts. This policy explains what information is collected or processed, how it is used, how it is stored and protected, and the privacy choices available to users.

Information We Collect Or Process

  • Authentication and session information, including Apple Sign In identifiers and backend session records.
  • Consent records, such as the consent type, consent version, source, status, timestamp, and non-secret disclosure metadata.
  • Plaid Link metadata, such as linked item, institution, and connection details needed to maintain account connectivity.
  • Liability information returned by Plaid, such as balances, statement amounts, payment due dates, account names, and related credit fields.
  • Connected checking and cash-management account metadata, balance information, selected-account references, and balance timestamps returned by Plaid when you authorize a cash-flow starting balance connection. TYDES uses this feature to populate Cash Flow starting balances and does not display transaction line items.
  • User-entered planning information, such as manual accounts, recurring events, cash-flow settings, and bill status entries.
  • Operational and security logs, such as request paths, timestamps, error categories, and hashed identifiers used for investigation and reliability.

Information Sources

  • Information you enter directly in the app.
  • Information generated when you accept a TYDES consent disclosure before connecting through Plaid Link.
  • Information returned by Plaid after you authorize a financial institution connection through Plaid Link.
  • Information from Apple Sign In used to authenticate your account.
  • Technical information generated by the app, backend, hosting provider, and security logs.

How We Use Information

  • To create and exchange Plaid Link tokens and maintain linked financial connections.
  • To record and honor your consent choices for Plaid-powered account connectivity.
  • To retrieve, store, and display liabilities and due-date information in the app.
  • To populate Cash Flow starting balances from authorized checking or cash-management account balances without displaying transaction line items.
  • To support planning features and cross-device planning-state sync for signed-in users.
  • To support security monitoring, fraud prevention, debugging, incident response, and service reliability.
  • To comply with legal, security, platform, and regulatory obligations when applicable.

Data Sharing and Service Providers

TYDES uses Plaid to support financial account connectivity. Information flow between financial institutions, Plaid, and TYDES is governed by user consent, platform terms, and applicable law. Before Plaid Link opens, TYDES asks you to consent to TYDES collecting, processing, and storing connected account and liability data for bill tracking and cash-flow forecasting. Plaid also presents its own consent and authorization flow inside Plaid Link.

Backend hosting, source-code hosting, app platform, and support providers may process limited operational data needed to provide, secure, troubleshoot, and maintain the service.

We do not sell personal information, rent personal information, or use Plaid-derived financial data for third-party advertising.

Data Retention

Data is retained for operational, security, and legal purposes for the period reasonably needed to provide the service. Authentication sessions expire after a limited period, linked-item metadata is kept while the institution remains connected, consent records are kept while the account remains active, planning-state data is kept while the account remains active, and liabilities history is retained on a rolling basis unless the user disconnects or requests deletion.

If a user disconnects a linked Plaid item through app controls, TYDES attempts to remove the Plaid Item through Plaid before deleting local linked-item records, encrypted Plaid access tokens, liability snapshots, liabilities history, and related coverage records from active backend storage.

If a verified user deletion request is completed, active app data associated with the account is deleted from TYDES active systems, including active consent records. Vendor backups, security logs, and workflow artifacts may age out under normal platform retention windows unless a legal, fraud-prevention, or security exception applies.

Some records may be retained for a limited period when needed for security, fraud prevention, legal compliance, backup integrity, dispute resolution, or incident investigation.

Your Privacy Choices

  • You can disconnect linked financial institutions from within the app experience.
  • You can stop future Plaid data collection by disconnecting linked institutions or requesting account-data deletion.
  • You can manage certain Plaid-side connection and deletion controls through Plaid Portal.
  • You can request privacy support, access clarification, correction help, or account-data deletion help through the project support channel listed below.

For request handling details, see Privacy Choices.

Do not post sensitive personal or financial information in public support messages. If needed, request a private contact path for identity verification and request handling.

Security

  • Transport security uses HTTPS/TLS.
  • Authentication uses Apple Sign In and backend session controls.
  • Plaid access tokens are encrypted at rest in backend storage.
  • Session tokens are stored as hashes, and security logs are designed not to include raw Plaid payloads or secrets.
  • Access to production systems is reviewed under TYDES access-control and vulnerability-management procedures.

Children

TYDES is not intended for children under 13.

Policy Updates
